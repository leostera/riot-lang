# Zort's Backlog

Add future `zort` work here.

This is a date-free execution plan with work split into immediate (`now`), shortly-after (`next`), and planned-but-not-in-cycle (`later`).

Use this order: fill `now` first, then pull from `next`, then `later` only when the `next` lane is healthy.

Each item is capability-level and includes:
- why this is needed for zort's goals,
- what capability is currently missing,
- and what cannot be achieved yet under the current architecture.

## now

- [ ] [P0] Managed-stack continuation growth, copying, and overflow semantics. Why: managed-stack payloads now exist, but multicore fibers still need explicit growth and failure behavior; What we need: copy/grow rules for suspended stacks, overflow policy, and ownership transfer semantics when continuations move between domains; Cannot do yet: trustworthy stack-heavy effects or system-stack replacement while continuation stacks remain a thin scaffold.
- [ ] [P0] Real parallel stop-the-world handshakes between domains, scheduler, and collector. Why: placeholder STW hooks are not enough once domains execute concurrently; What we need: cooperative safepoints, pause acknowledgements, and progress guarantees that work while fibers are running or parked; Cannot do yet: parallel collection or safe multicore scheduling while STW is still a single-threaded coordinator.
- [ ] [P0] Work-stealing and cross-domain fiber migration policy. Why: multicore fibers require load balancing after local run queues exist; What we need: stealable queues, migration-safe ownership rules, and explicit support for suspending in one domain and resuming in another; Cannot do yet: safe multicore scheduling or fair load distribution while migration policy is implicit.
- [ ] [P0] Effect/fiber/domain stress suite. Why: the new priority lane needs hostile tests before we add more parallel behavior; What we need: nested effects, parking/unparking, cross-domain resumption, domain attach/detach churn, and GC under heavy suspension; Cannot do yet: safe iteration on multicore semantics without confidence under stress.
- [ ] [P0] Shared-memory-safe atomic field and scheduler coordination primitives. Why: domains and multicore fibers need a real low-level concurrency substrate; What we need: atomic field access/update semantics, queue coordination primitives, and explicit memory-ordering policy; Cannot do yet: correct lock-free scheduling or cross-domain queue ownership.
- [ ] [P0] Signal ingress + callback scheduling from host to runtime services. Why: zort needs deterministic external-event handling for parity-like behavior; What we need: native signal capture, queueing, and delivery to `RuntimeServices`; Cannot do yet: true OS-level signal fidelity across all target platforms without full platform abstraction work.
- [ ] [P0] Owned alternate signal-stack lifecycle control. Why: safe signal handling requires stack policy ownership and correct unwind behavior; What we need: setup/restore/teardown ownership records and coexistence handling with foreign stacks; Cannot do yet: platform-portable stack-stack semantics until we finalize per-platform backends.
- [ ] [P0] Deterministic pending-action drain at safe scheduling points. Why: callback-driven features (signals/finalizers) must not starve behind normal execution; What we need: unified drain checkpoints and ordering rules for blocking transitions; Cannot do yet: full preemption or thread handoff semantics before scheduling/domain infrastructure is in place.
- [ ] [P1] Uniform callback-boundary mediation for all external entry/exit paths. Why: effects and callbacks must be consistently isolated from ambient runtime state; What we need: boundary checks at every C/API ingress and egress path used by callback delivery; Cannot do yet: exhaustive full C API parity until the shim/API surface is stabilized.
- [ ] [P1] Clarified policy for service-name storage and synchronization (`named` values/handlers). Why: consistency of compatibility state is required before adding plugin, callback, and debugger features; What we need: explicit ownership and synchronization policy documented and test-locked; Cannot do yet: cross-process/global sharing guarantees while runtime instances remain intentionally isolated.
- [ ] [P1] Blocking-path correctness tests for mixed pending callbacks. Why: this is the safety gate for `now`; What we need: tests asserting one-shot draining, no duplicate execution, and stable ordering; Cannot do yet: comprehensive cross-runtime stress coverage without broader test harness for async external events.

## next

- [ ] [P1] Retire the `orphan_fibers` GC fallback with strict scheduler ownership. Why: the current fallback preserves safety while direct control-kernel paths still exist, but it hides architecture drift; What we need: every live fiber either owned by a scheduler/domain lane or explicitly rejected, plus tests that bypass paths fail loudly instead of relying on fallback rooting; Cannot do yet: clean ownership proofs while low-level direct activation paths remain public and in use.
- [ ] [P1] Domain startup/shutdown and worker ownership semantics. Why: multicore runtime usability depends on deterministic worker lifecycle and clean teardown; What we need: domain bootstrap, shutdown ordering, parked-work draining, and idempotent worker exit rules; Cannot do yet: reliable embedded/runtime-managed worker pools while lifecycle remains implicit.
- [ ] [P1] Typed plugin registration contract and required-metadata validation policy. Why: a typed contract enables safe optional dynlink while avoiding raw native-link assumptions; What we need: explicit metadata schema, required symbol checks, and deterministic failure shapes; Cannot do yet: complete binary loader parity until we define supported plugin formats in runtime policy.
- [ ] [P1] Compatibility GC-control façade for lifecycle operations and policy knobs. Why: user-facing GC control should be explicit without binding semantics to collector internals; What we need: boundary-facing commands mapped to collector hooks and invariants; Cannot do yet: direct OCaml API parity for all undocumented legacy fields.
- [ ] [P1] Memory-management tuning and runtime-parameter exposure surface. Why: visibility into collector behavior is required for benchmarking and debugging; What we need: stable configuration surface and diagnostics rendering; Cannot do yet: exact numeric compatibility for all vendor/runtime parameters while we intentionally keep knobs subsetted.
- [ ] [P1] Memory-profiling callback pipeline and filtering. Why: callback-based profiling enables low-overhead integration with tooling; What we need: registration lifecycle, payload schema, and filtering strategy by site/category; Cannot do yet: full low-level integration with external profilers until sink formats are standardized.
- [ ] [P1] Structural compare/hash baseline in compatibility layer. Why: typed comparisons are core language observability and deterministic hashing behavior; What we need: invalid-value matrix, traversal budgets, and traversal interruption strategy; Cannot do yet: exhaustive parity for every custom callback mode and all historical edge-cases.
- [ ] [P1] Boundary split for runtime-hosted services. Why: keeps core semantic runtime small and maintainable; What we need: explicit module boundary for channels/sync/io-like capabilities and compatibility contract; Cannot do yet: complete host-primitive replacement until feature owners are agreed.
- [ ] [P1] Aggregated memory-profiling summaries by site/object class. Why: users need actionable summaries beyond raw samples; What we need: aggregation counters, export format, and summary reports; Cannot do yet: long-term stability guarantees for report schema before storage/versioning policy is set.
- [ ] [P1] Deletion-safe root-provider iteration. Why: roots can be updated during traversal in realistic workloads; What we need: tombstone/deferred-removal model with traversal invariants; Cannot do yet: full concurrent GC-thread safety model before multi-domain execution exists.
- [ ] [P1] Runtime-owned synchronization primitives with ownership and error mapping. Why: external code requires deterministic lock-like behavior without exposing raw runtime internals; What we need: first-class resource handles, lifecycle rules, and mapping to canonical exceptions; Cannot do yet: full fairness/perf tuning without dedicated sync stress harness.
- [ ] [P1] Expanded observability with regression-backed metrics artifacts. Why: changes in GC/control semantics need measurable validation; What we need: artifact schema, counters, and targeted regression tests; Cannot do yet: long-horizon dashboard conventions and governance for historical baselines.
- [ ] [P1] Generational mutation stress suite for remembered-edge correctness. Why: nursery/major boundaries are correctness-critical as code scales; What we need: mutation-heavy directed tests and stronger edge coverage; Cannot do yet: full production workload corpus for confidence before next collector milestones.
- [ ] [P1] Hardened external-result contracts and API boundary typing. Why: boundary consumers need stable semantics instead of internal heap encoding leakage; What we need: explicit result/error contract and migration away from legacy encoded assumptions; Cannot do yet: complete end-to-end migration of every external consumer path.
